## Background

Ventricular tachycardia (VT) is a life-threatening arrhythmia that is difficult to treat safely with conventional therapies. Stereotactic arrhythmia radioablation (STAR) offers a non-invasive alternative, delivering ablative radiation doses to the arrhythmogenic substrate. However, the heart moves continuously with both cardiac contraction and respiration, and accurate real-time tracking of the target is essential to avoid delivering dose to surrounding healthy tissue.

## Aims

- Develop a real-time tracking system for cardiac and respiratory motion during non-invasive STAR.
- Improve treatment precision by enabling accurate radiation delivery to a moving cardiac target.
- Reduce treatment-related complications and improve patient outcomes.
